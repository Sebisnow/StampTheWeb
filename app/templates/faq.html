What is StampTheWeb?

StampTheWeb is a free Trusted timestamping service for web content. Trusted timestamping is the process of securely keeping track of the creation and modification time of a document. This system creates timestamps of the requested web pages. To find more about Trusted timestamps visit its Wikipedia Page.

Use Cases

Proving an information online existed at a certain point in time.
Comparing online information with a version from past.
Finding if changes occur in online news pages with time.
Proving an idea or information published online at a certain date and time.

Trusted timestamps for offline digital documents can also be created using OriginStamp

Who created and inspires StampTheWeb

The idea for this project was provided by Prof. Bela Gipp from the Information Science Group at the University of Konstanz. The implementation started as a students project in Prof. Gipp's course Web Information Retrieval 2015/2016. The group of students, Manfred Schäfer, Tobias Bamberger and Sebastian Schneider layed the foundations (including the fabulous name StampTheWeb) and the first implementation for timestamping websites.
Furthermore StampTheWeb was adopted by Waqar Detho and Sebastian Schneider as part of a Master and Bachelor thesis at the University of Konstanz shaping the webservice to what it is now. The project was supervised by the doctoral student Corinna Breitinger.
The service of this site is based on the originstamp.org service and uses the OriginStamp API, which were developed by André Gernandt, Thomas Hepp and Bela Gipp.

How does it work?

StampTheWeb extracts data from the requested web pages and calculates its hash and then creates timestamp for the hash which represents timestamp for the web content. Furthermore this timestamp and hash is aggregated to Bitcoin block chain network, by performing a Bitcoin transaction. This process makes it secure, since hash is always present in the distributed Bitcoin block chain network and could be verified from Bitcoin.


1) When a user submits a URL, 'StampTheWeb' extracts the URL from the internet and extracts the text from that URL and calculates hash from the text.
2) ‘StampTheWeb’ embeds this hash to the Bitcoin blockchain.
3) The hash is forever embedded in Bitcoin blockchain and can be verified by using blockchain.info or using blockchain itself or by using OriginStamp

Why can’t the timestamp be manipulated?

This timestamping services uses the cryptographic blockchain of the virtual currency bitcoin. The blockchain was designed to be tamper proof. If they could be manipulated than the whole currency would be worthless. In addition to the blockchain OriginStamp also publish the hash immediately on twitter

What services StampTheWeb offers?

Stamps web articles, news articles or blog posts etc
Compare two saved (timestamped) web pages with each other or with the current online version.
Compare a timestamped article with the same article from any other part of the world for e.g. Russia, UK, USA etc.
Compare a timestamped article from a certain date with its current online version.
Compare a timestamped article from a certain date with its timestamped version from another date.
Check if an online article is blocked in any other part of the world.
Check where in the world an article is blocked through a map.
Review statistics of web pages most visited in the system with respect to country of origin.
Time-stamp an article according to a user-defined schedule. If any change is detected in that article the user is notified by email (if email is provided), or information is automatically stored in the system.
Time-stamp an article according to a user-defined schedule and compare it at regular intervals with the same page from other countries. The user is notified by email if changes are found.

How schedule recurring URLs work?

‘StampTheWeb’ creates the timestamp of the provided URL and then frequently checks the article and compares it with the timestamped version. If ‘StampTheWeb’ finds a change in the version at the remote location then it will notify the user by email (if email is provide). In other way it will create another timestamp of the provided URL.

How schedule recurring URLs from different locations work?

‘StampTheWeb’ creates the timestamp of the provided URL and then frequently checks the article on the provided location (for example China) and compares it with the timestamped version. If ‘StampTheWeb’ finds a change in the version at the remote location then it will notify the user by email (if email is provide). In other way it will create another timestamp of the provided URL.
