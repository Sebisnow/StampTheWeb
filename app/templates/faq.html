What is StampTheWeb?

StampTheWeb is a free Trusted time-stamping service for online web content. Trusted time-stamping is the process of securely keeping track of the creation and modification time of a document. This system creates time-stamps of the requested web pages. To find more about Trusted time-stamps visit its Wikipedia Page.

Use Cases

Proving an information online existed at a certain point in time.
Comparing online information with a version from past.
Finding if changes occur in online news pages with time.
Proving an idea or information published online at a certain date and time.

Trusted time-stamps for offline digital documents can also be created using OriginStamp

How does it work?

StampTheWeb extracts data from the requested web pages and calculates its hash and then creates time-stamp for the hash which represents time-stamp for the web content. Furthermore this time-stamp and hash is aggregated to Bitcoin block chain network, by performing a Bitcoin transaction. This process makes it secure, since hash is always present in the distributed Bitcoin block chain network and could be verified from Bitcoin.


1) When you submits a URL 'StampTheWeb' extracts the URL from the internet and extracts the text from that URL and calculates hash from the text.
2) StampTheWeb submit this hash to the Bitcoin Blockchain.
3) The hash is forever embedded in Bitcoin Blockchian and can be verified by using blockchain.info or using blockchain itself or by using StampTheWeb's parent system OriginStamp

Why can’t the time-stamp be manipulated?

This time-stamping services uses the cryptographic blockchain of the virtual currency bitcoin. The blockchain was designed to be tamper proof. If they could be manipulated than the whole currency would be worthless. In addition to the blockchain OriginStamp also publish the hash immediately on twitter

What services StampTheWeb offers?

Stamps web articles, news articles or blog posts etc
Compare two saved (time-stamped) web pages with each other or with the current online version.
Compare a time-stamped article with the same article from any other part of the world for e.g. Russia, UK, USA etc.
Compare a time-stamped article from a certain date with its current online version.
Compare a time-stamped article from a certain date with its time-stamped version from another date.
Check if an online article is blocked in any other part of the world.
Check where in the world an article is blocked through a map.
Review statistics of web pages most visited in the system with respect to country of origin.
Time-stamp an article according to a user-defined schedule. If any change is detected in that article the user is notified by email (if email is provided), or information is automatically stored in the system.
Time-stamp an article according to a user-defined schedule and compare it at regular intervals with the same page from other countries. The user is notified by email if changes are found.
